  • 5. THE SPACE BETWEEN STAR SYSTEMS IS HUGE! Google Maps Astronomers measure distances to some nearby stars using light years, the distance light travels in one Earth year. Light travels at 186,262 miles per second, a light year is about 5,900,000,000,000 miles! Our closest neighbors, the Centauri system, are a little over 4 light years away.

  • 7. Gather your supplies! 4 to 12 pieces of recycled office paper Star print out (LINK BELOW, colored, or color your own!) Scissors Clear tape Measuring tape or ruler Pen String (I use embroidery floss, 30 pieces cut to 18 inch lengths) My string colors roughly correspond to the star colors, but this is not necessary.

  • 9. 11.5 inches Roll up a piece of office paper tightly along the long (11.5 inch) edge and tape up the middle and ends. Hint: rolling the paper around a pencil or pen (without a clip) really helps! Tape! Tape! Tape!
  • 10. Make 3 more cylinders.
  • 11. Tape together 2 cylinders in an L shape. Tape both sides for stability
  • 12. Tape together the remaining cylinders to complete a square. Note the alternating corners!
  • 13. Label the lower left corner with x and y axes and arrows. This step is extremely important!
  • 14. Measure 5 inches from the lower left corner along the x axis and draw a line on the paper.
  • 15. Measure 5 inches from the lower left corner along the y axis and draw a line on the paper.
  • 16. Loop the string around the mark along the x axis and tie a double knot.
  • 17. Pull the string straight across to the other side, loop the string around, and tie a double knot.
  • 18. Repeat the previous 2 steps with a string at the y axis mark. I wrap the second string around the first string where they intersect.
  • 19. Tie a third string at the intersection of the first two strings.
  • 20. Measure this string 5 inches from the knot and cut off the rest of the string.
  • 21. Cut out your Sun and tape the back side to the end of the string near the middle of the Sun.
  • 22. (Optional, but recommended) Cut out the Sun label (or make your own!) and tape it to the string above the Sun.
  • 23. Cool! Your model is already coming along nicely!
  • 24. To keep things clean, I like to cut off the string ends, leaving about inch of slack.
  • 25. Most of the remaining steps are the same as for the Sun, so for the rest of the systems I will give x and y positions for the strings attached to the frame, and the string length for the star system like this: Centauri A, Centauri B, Proxima Centauri x y string 4 inches 5 inches 8 inches

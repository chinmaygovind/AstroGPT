Inspired by Reesecandy2003 EvanVizuett, Rebus2077, Matusurvive, SC2HayasuiArts, and Me!

According to NASA's, Transiting Planets and Planetesimals Small Telescope (TRAPPIST), La Silla Observatory description, the TRAPPIST-1 system, Originally has 7 exoplanets of the TRAPPIST-1's Solar System, but has 10 or more planets instead. I've expanded My Headcanon of the Taur Force 64 Universe added planets and names for these planets which it reminds me V1 of Tangea's Planetary System from Buzz Lightyear of Star Command.

Planets of the TRAPPIST-1 system.
TRAPPIST-1 (2MASS J23062928-0502285): A Red dwarf star, in the constellation Aquarius with a planetary system of seven known planets. Its mass is about 9% of the Sun's, with a radius slightly larger than the planet Jupiter and a surface temperature of about 2,566 K (2,293 Â°C). The star is 40.7 light-years (12.5 pc) from the Sun and is estimated to be 7.6 billion years old, making it older than the Solar System. 

Xemiu (Known as TRAPPIST-1a): A Molten Mercury-like planet closer to TRAPPIST-1

Damu (Known as TRAPPIST-1b): A Green Venus-like planet also designated as 2MASS J23062928-0502285 b, is a mainly rocky, Venus-like exoplanet orbiting around the ultra-cool dwarf star Ocraguras (TRAPPIST-1), located approximately 40 light-years (12 parsecs) away from Earth in the constellation of Aquarius. The planet was detected using the transit method, where a planet dims the host star's light as it passes in front of it. It was first announced on May 2, 2016, and between 2017 and 2018, more studies were able to refine its physical parameters.

The planet is about the same mass as Earth but about 12% larger. Its relatively low density, along with spectroscopic observations, has confirmed an extremely thick and hot atmosphere. Observations published in 2018 showed that the atmosphere of TRAPPIST-1b was much larger than that of Earth or Venus, as well as being very hot and potentially rich in CO2.[2] More recent modeling studies have suggested the planet is too hot to allow the formation of sulfuric acid clouds, such as are found on Venus, the hottest planet in our solar system.

Aja (Known as TRAPPIST-1c): An Orange and Brown Venus-like planet also designated as 2MASS J23062928-0502285 c, is a mainly rocky, Venus-like exoplanet orbiting around the ultracool dwarf star Ocraguras (TRAPPIST-1) approximately 40 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Aquarius. It is the most massive and third largest planet of the system, with about 116% the mass and 110% the radius of Earth. Its density indicates a primarily rocky composition with a very thick Venus-like atmosphere, although it is expected to be thinner than that of Damu (TRAPPIST-1b).

Huanca (Known as TRAPPIST-1d): An Earth-like Exoplanet with Jungles, Rainforests, Green Blue Plants, Swamps, Marshes and Insect-like Alien Lifeforms. These Insectoids have their Centaur-like Body, 4 Arms and Crab-like hands who worked for Queen Cosmetta on her homeworld due to slavery. Huanca is Located in the Gamma Quadrant of Sector 18 (Like in Buzz Lightyear of Star Command, Buzz Lightyear's Astro Blasters, Lightyear (2022)) from the Milky Way Galaxy and it is the Homeworld of the Insectoids.
-Huanca Minor: A Luna-like Moon with Moon Bases, Hangars, Cities and Defense Bases

Ruaumoko (Known as TRAPPIST-1e): An Earth-like Planet with Octopus-like Alien Lifeforms with 20 Eyes, Purple Skin and 10 Tentacles. it is also designated as 2MASS J23062928-0502285 e, is a solid, close-to-Earth-sized exoplanet orbiting within the habitable zone around the ultracool dwarf star Ocraguras (TRAPPIST-1) approximately 40 light-years (12 parsecs; 380 trillion kilometers; 240 trillion miles) away from Earth in the constellation of Aquarius. Astronomers found the exoplanet by using the transit method, in which the dimming effect that a planet causes as it crosses in front of its star is measured.

The exoplanet was one of seven new exoplanets discovered orbiting the star using observations from the Spitzer Space Telescope.[4][5] Three of the seven (e, f, and g) are in the habitable zone.[6][7] TRAPPIST-1e is similar to Earth's mass, radius, density, gravity, temperature, and stellar flux.[1][2] It is also confirmed to not have a cloud-free hydrogen-dominated atmosphere, meaning it is more likely to have a compact atmosphere like the terrestrial planets in our solar system.[8]

In November 2018, researchers determined that of the seven exoplanets in the multi-planetary system, TRAPPIST-1e has the best chance of being an Earth-like ocean planet, and the one most worthy of further study regarding habitability.[9] Furthermore, according to the Habitable Exoplanets Catalog, TRAPPIST-1e is one of the most potentially habitable exoplanets discovered.

Asintmah (Known as TRAPPIST-1f): A PNF-404-like Exoplanet also designated as 2MASS J23062928-0502285 f, is an exoplanet, likely rocky but under a massive water-steam gaseous envelope at very high pressure and temperature,[1] orbiting within the habitable zone[2] around the ultracool dwarf star Ocraguras (TRAPPIST-1) 40 light-years (12 parsecs) away from Earth in the constellation of Aquarius. The exoplanet was found by using the transit method, in which the dimming effect that a planet causes as it crosses in front of its star is measured.

Ostara (Known as TRAPPIST-1g): A Terrestrial planet also designated as 2MASS J23062928-0502285 g and K2-112 g, is an exoplanet orbiting around the ultra-cool dwarf star Ocraguras (TRAPPIST-1) 39 light-years (12 parsecs) away from Earth in the constellation Aquarius. It was one of four new exoplanets to be discovered orbiting the star using observations from the Spitzer Space Telescope.[4] The exoplanet is within the optimistic habitable zone of its host star.[5] It was found by using the transit method, in which the dimming effect that a planet causes as it crosses in front of its star is measured.

The second most distant known planet in its system, TRAPPIST-1g is a planet larger than Earth yet less dense, meaning it likely hosts some form of water.

Aranyani (Known as TRAPPIST-1h): A Mercury-like planet also designated as 2MASS J23062928-0502285 h, is an exoplanet orbiting around the ultra-cool dwarf star Ocraguras (TRAPPIST-1) 39 light-years (12 parsecs) away from Earth in the constellation Aquarius. It was one of four new exoplanets to be discovered orbiting the star using observations from the Spitzer Space Telescope.[4][5] Throughout 2017 and 2018, more studies were able to refine its physical parameters.

The outermost known planet in its system, it is roughly one third the mass of Earth, and about 77% as large. Its relatively low density indicates that it is likely water-rich, like several other planets in the system.

Lozor (Known as TRAPPIST-1i): A Red Mars-like planet with planetary ringed system and 3 Moons.


Yoshi'upiter (Known as TRAPPIST-1j): A Bright Green Jupiter-like Gas Giant with 88 Moons. It is a Mix between these names are Yoshi from Super Mario World and Jupiter is the 5th planet from the sun

-Ziguray: A Spring Green Triton-like Moon
-Carut: A Red Titan-like Moon

Yoh-Tay (Known as TRAPPIST-1j): A Magenta Saturn-like Gas Giant with Planetary Ringed System, Thunderstorms, Supersonic Winds, Heavy Rainfalls and 69 Moons.
